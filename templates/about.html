Lip reading is used to understand or interpret speech without hearing it, a technique especially mastered by people with hearing difficulties. It is well known that the use of visual cites improves the recognition results especially in scenarios with high level of acoustical noise. Likewise we can use morse code to detect eyes and convert it into text. Morse code is a method of transmitting text information as a series of on-off tones, lights, or clicks or in some cases eyes blink that can be directly understood by a skilled listener or observer without special equipment. It is named for Samuel F. B. Morse, an inventor of the telegraph.
